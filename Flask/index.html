Cancer Classification


Cancer is a complex group of diseases characterized by the abnormal
and uncontrollable growth of cells that can form tumors and invade
surrounding tissues. It encompasses over 100 different types, each
with distinct characteristics and behaviors. Causes of cancer include
genetic factors, environmental exposures, and viral infections. Early detection through screenings and awareness of symptoms is crucial
for effective treatment. Treatment modalities, such as surgery, and
chemotherapy, radiation therapy, immunotherapy, and targeted
therapy, vary depending on the cancer type and stage. Lifestyle changes,
vaccinations, and screening programs play roles in prevention.
Ongoing research contributes to advancements in cancer understanding, diagnosis, and treatment, offering hope for improved outcomes.
